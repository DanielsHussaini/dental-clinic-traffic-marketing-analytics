# Dental Clinic Patient Traffic & Marketing Analytics

## Project Overview

This project analyzes patient traffic and acquisition patterns for a multi-branch dental clinic in Nigeria. The goal was to help clinic management understand how patients are being acquired, which branch is performing better across different channels, and how marketing efforts can be optimized to increase patient volume.

The analysis focuses on patient traffic trends, branch-level performance, seasonal movement, and marketing channel contribution. Revenue data was excluded from the project scope, so the core business objective was patient volume growth rather than income analysis.

## Business Problem

The clinic wanted to better understand:

* Which branch attracts more patient traffic
* Which marketing and acquisition channels drive the highest patient volume
* Whether patient traffic follows seasonal patterns
* Which channels are growing or declining over time
* How management can use data to make better marketing and operational decisions

Before this project, traffic and acquisition insights were not fully structured for strategic decision-making. This project turned raw patient records into a clear business intelligence report.

## Project Goals

The main goals of this project were to:

1. Analyze annual patient traffic across both branches.
2. Compare Jabi and Apo branch performance.
3. Identify peak and low-traffic months.
4. Rank the strongest patient acquisition channels.
5. Track how traffic sources changed between 2024 and 2025.
6. Generate practical recommendations for marketing and business development.
7. Build a healthcare analytics portfolio project using real business data.

## Dataset Summary

The analysis was based on aggregated patient folder records from two clinic branches:

* Jabi Branch
* Apo Branch

The dataset covered patient traffic from 2022 to 2025.

Key dataset highlights:

* 9,382 total patient records analyzed
* 3,441 Jabi branch patients
* 4,691 Apo branch patients
* 2,894 patients recorded in 2025, the strongest combined year
* Revenue data was excluded from the analysis
* Unknown channel entries were excluded from source ranking analysis

No personally identifiable patient information was used in this public portfolio version.

## Key Analysis Areas

### 1. Annual Traffic Analysis

The report compared yearly patient volume between the Jabi and Apo branches.

Key findings:

* Apo grew by 143% from 2022 to 2025.
* Jabi grew by 113% from 2023 to 2024.
* 2025 was the strongest combined traffic year.
* Jabi experienced an 8% decline in 2025 compared to 2024.

This helped identify both long-term growth and areas requiring management attention.

### 2. Seasonal Patient Traffic Patterns

Monthly traffic was analyzed to identify periods of high and low patient demand.

Key findings:

* Jabi recorded its all-time monthly high in August 2025 with 233 patients.
* Both branches showed strong performance in August.
* Apo showed steady growth through 2025.
* Jabi and Apo both experienced certain low-traffic periods that can guide campaign timing.

This insight is useful for planning staffing, marketing pushes, promotions, and appointment capacity.

### 3. Traffic Channel Ranking

Patient acquisition sources were ranked across both branches.

Top combined traffic channels included:

1. Walk-ins
2. Google
3. Referrals
4. Insurance / HMO
5. Instagram
6. Friend / Family
7. Returning clients
8. Signage / Billboard
9. Facebook
10. Leadway Insurance

Key finding:

Walk-ins were the strongest overall traffic source, while Google was the strongest digital acquisition channel.

### 4. Branch-Level Channel Comparison

The project compared which branch led each acquisition channel.

Key findings:

* Apo led in walk-ins, referrals, HMO patients, Instagram, signage, Facebook, and Leadway Insurance.
* Jabi led in Google traffic and returning clients.
* Apo’s street visibility appears to be stronger.
* Jabi shows strong digital discovery and repeat-client potential.

This suggests that both branches require different marketing strategies.

### 5. Channel Trends Over Time

The project compared how key traffic sources changed from 2024 to 2025.

Key findings:

* Jabi referrals grew from 61 in 2024 to 139 in 2025.
* Apo referrals grew from 52 in 2024 to 209 in 2025.
* Google remained a major digital driver across both branches.
* Walk-ins remained the strongest source of patient acquisition.
* Insurance / HMO traffic was much stronger in Apo than Jabi.

This showed that referral growth is one of the clinic’s strongest organic signals.

## Strategic Insights

### 1. Walk-ins remain the clinic’s strongest acquisition source

Across both branches, walk-ins generated the highest number of patients. This suggests that location visibility, signage, physical accessibility, and neighborhood awareness are major growth drivers.

### 2. Google is the strongest digital channel

Google generated 982 patients across both branches. This makes it the most important digital channel and a strong candidate for further investment in local SEO, Google Business Profile optimization, and review generation.

### 3. Referrals are growing rapidly

Referral growth was one of the strongest signals in the data:

* Jabi referrals grew by 128%
* Apo referrals grew by 302%

This suggests that patient satisfaction and word-of-mouth are already working and can be formalized into a structured referral program.

### 4. Apo has stronger HMO performance

Apo recorded significantly more Insurance / HMO patients than Jabi. This reveals an opportunity for Jabi to develop more HMO and corporate partnerships.

### 5. August is a critical capacity planning month

Both branches showed strong August performance, with Jabi reaching its all-time high in August 2025. Management should prepare for August demand with staffing, appointment planning, and targeted campaigns.

### 6. Each branch needs a different marketing strategy

Apo performs strongly through walk-ins, referrals, HMO, and offline visibility. Jabi shows strong Google performance and returning-client potential. A single marketing strategy for both branches would miss important branch-level differences.

## Recommendations

### 1. Formalize the referral program

Because referrals grew strongly in both branches, the clinic should create a structured referral system. This may include thank-you messages, referral tracking, patient appreciation, or simple incentives.

### 2. Expand HMO and corporate partnerships at Jabi

Apo has much stronger HMO performance. Jabi can grow patient traffic by registering with more HMO providers and targeting nearby organizations.

### 3. Double down on Google and local SEO

Google is the top digital channel. The clinic should optimize its Google Business Profile, collect more reviews, update service pages, and improve local search visibility.

### 4. Plan ahead for August demand

August should be treated as a high-demand month. The clinic can prepare by adjusting schedules, increasing appointment capacity, planning campaigns earlier, and ensuring enough clinical support.

### 5. Improve channel tracking

To improve future analysis, the clinic should standardize how patient source information is collected. Every new patient should be asked, “How did you hear about us?” and responses should be recorded consistently.
